Sunday, September 26th, 1999
The House of My Father
"Do Not Fear"
Psalm 23:6

The Lord loves you.
The House of My Father
Eden was the extremely beautiful
place that the Father made for
Adam and Eve. It was equipped 
with everything such as dwelling 
places, gardens, orchards and 
so on. Driven out of there,
Adam and Eve had to build a 
house to dwell by their hands.
In the primitive age, people made
caves and built houses with leaves 
and grass, where they lived.
If we had no houses to dwell 
in while living in this world,
we'd be awfully uneasy and sad.
That's why people make every
effort and have strong desires
to have their own houses. Yet, 
no matter how beautiful our 
houses may be in this world,
we have to leave them forever
in less than 100 years. Death 
will relentlessly expel us 
from our places. If we are 
driven out of earthly tents,
then where shall we go?
Luke 16:19-31 tells us of 
a rich man and Lazarus, 
"There was a rich man who was 
dressed in purple and fine linen
and lived in luxury every day."
Purple dress symbolizes authority, 
and fine linen, great wealth.
The rich man had a nice house,
abundant materials, and he was
dressed in fine robes enjoying
all kinds of pleasures. But he
was indifferent to and ignorant
of heaven and he didn't prepare
anything for that at all. He had 
everything that would pass away 
but nothing for heaven. At his
gate was laid a beggar named
Lazarus. Since the beggar had 
no house, he lived at the gate 
of the rich man's house.
He had nothing, so he ate what
fell from the rich man's table.
He was wearing rags and
covered with sores. Even the
dogs came and licked his sores.
Although he had nothing in 
the world, he had hope and 
faith for heaven, As time went
by, both the rich man and the
beggar faced death. We read
in Luke 16:22, "The time came
when the beggar died and the
angels carried him to Abraham's 
side. The rich man also died 
and was buried." Lazarus' death
was neglected. He was dying
for water but no one gave him
a drop of water. He took his 
last breath all alone. After he
died, people put him on a mat
and threw him into the cemetery.
However, the Bible doesn't say
that he disappeared but it says
the angels carried him to 
Abraham's side. He entered 
where there were splendid houses,
fine linen and places overflowing
with happiness. He had nothing
on this earth, but when he left
this earth, the Lord had prepared
a beautiful heaven where he 
went and enjoyed extreme joy.  
The rich man also died as time
went by. People prepared a 
tremendous funeral, decorated his 
coffin with flowers and carried 
him to his grave, where crowds 
of mourners gathered. Yet, the 
Scripture does not say that he 
was there. It says that he fell
into hell and was suffering from
fire, where there was no house,
clothes, nor happiness. It was
our Lord, Jesus Christ, who said
that. Luke 16:23-26 reads, "In hell,
where he was in torment, he 
looked up and saw Abraham far 
away, with Lazarus by his side. 
So he called to him, 'Father 
Abraham, have pity on me and 
send Lazarus to dip the tip of 
his finger in water and cool my
tongue, because I am in agony 
in this fire.' But Abraham 
replied, 'Son, remember that 
in your lifetime you received 
your good things, while Lazarus 
received bad things, but now 
he is comforted here and you 
are in agony. And besides all
this, between us and you a great
chasm has been fixed, so that 
those who want to go from here
to you cannot, nor can anyone
cross over from there to us.' "
The rich man thought that he
would live in luxury on earth
permanently but death drove him 
out of his beautiful and luxurious 
house. The beggar did not 
continue to suffer from poverty
and sickness. When he died, 
the angels carried him to eternal
heaven. Since we were made in
the likeness and image of God
as the Scripture says, "God is
Spirit, and His worshippers must
worship in spirit and in truth,"
our souls are covered with bodies
and if our bodies die, our spirits
will go away. That's why the
Bible says that our bodies are
tents. If the sun shines, those 
tents will be warm; if it is windy, 
they will blow; if it is cold, 
they will be cold. Likewise, our 
bodies are like tents controlled
by the environment. They will 
be worn out some day. Our 
bodily tents must be broken.    
I've heard of a story. A man 
and a woman were riding a boat
on upper stream of the Niagara 
Falls and they enjoyed themselves.
They had much food and wine 
and fell asleep. Since they were
on the upper part of the falls,
they thought they would be safe.
Yet, while sleeping, their boat 
ran along the water and got into
the Niagara Falls. When they 
were near the Falls, people around 
shouted, "It's dangerous. Wake up!"  
They were in deep sleep so 
they didn't hear but on feeling 
the boat shaking badly, they 
awoke but it was too late.
The boat swiftly fell into 
Niagara Falls and both of them
died. Life is like riding the boat 
of time. In a very moment, we 
will experience our boats' falling 
into the cliff of death. Therefore,
Jesus Himself says that wealth,
fame and splendor in this world 
will never be forever, and neither 
will starvation, hunger and poverty.
He is warning that unless we 
are aware of heaven and hell 
and are ready, we will lose 
the glory of heaven and fall 
into eternal hell. The Lord 
came to this earth to give us 
the eternal place. Why did the 
Lord leave the throne of heaven
and come to this earth as a man?
It's because there is heaven 
for us to take and there's hell 
for us to avoid. Heaven is so 
glorious and beautiful and hell 
is so fearful and troubling that 
the Lord came to warn us not 
to fall into hell. but to be saved 
and to go to heaven. John 14:1-3 
says, "Do not let your hearts 
be troubled. Trust in God; trust 
also in me. In my Father's house 
are many rooms; if it were not 
so, I would have told you. I am 
going there to prepare a place
for you. And if I go and prepare 
a place for you, I will come back 
and take you to be with me that 
you also may be where I am." 
What is more fearful than death 
is that there is no place to dwell 
after we cross over the river 
of death. When we leave our
physical tents, there will be no
place to go and stay. We'll be
abandoned and lost. That is 
more fearful. Yet, after we die,
there will be a beautiful land
over the river. We will go there
by faith. If we know that there
will be a beautiful and glorious
place, we don't have to be afraid
of death any longer. But after 
we die, if we were lost, forsaken,
and thrown into the place where
there would be no God and life, 
that would be most terrifying.
How miserable would we be 
if we fell into pitch-dark hell? 
In 2 Peter 2:17 we read, 
"Blackest darkness is reserved 
for them." If there were no light 
but darkness all around, how tragic 
would it be? Matthew 25:30 states,
"And throw that worthless servant 
outside, into the darkness, where 
there will be weeping and gnashing 
of teeth." The Bible says that he
will be thrown into the darkness
where there will be weeping and
gnashing of teeth. Why will there
be gnashing of teeth? Because
he will be tormented so severely.
Matthew 13:42 says, "They will
throw them into the fiery furnace." 
In the country, a smith puts 
the iron into the furnace and 
takes it out over again. Likewise, 
the Bible says that they will 
be thrown into such a fierce 
and hot fiery furnace in which 
the fire will not cease. Therefore, 
our Lord wants all of us to avoid 
that and to go to the Father's 
house where we will live forever. 
He wants us to enter the beautiful 
house that our Father in heaven 
prepared for us. Jesus already 
went there and made it with 
everything beautiful for us. 
We don't know how to get to 
the Father's house. Since Adam 
and Eve were driven out of Eden, 
we have been lost. We have 
lived with physical lust and greed 
in this world, just working on 
earth. Then how do we know 
the way to heaven? No matter
how hard people tried to find
the way to heaven, they just
faced the deep valley of sin and 
death. No one went to heaven 
through that valley. For those 
who fell into the valley all were 
destroyed. Yet, Jesus came and 
for our sakes, His body was 
torn and He shed His blood 
on the cross to bear our sins. 
After dying, He descended into
hell, arose on the third day,
destroyed the valley of sin and
death and became the way for 
us to cross over. Only Jesus is 
the way to go over the valley 
of sin and death. Apart from 
Jesus, we can't find the way
to cross over that valley. 
Jesus says, "I am the way and
the truth and the life. No one
comes to the Father except
through me." Peter says, "There 
is no other name under heaven 
given to men by which we must 
be saved." If we reject the way 
of Christ, we must fall into the 
valley of sin and death and be 
destroyed forever. Therefore, 
Jesus came to this earth to be
the way for you and me so 
that, through Him, we may 
escape destruction and enter 
into eternal heaven. That's why
He says, "God so loved the world
that He gave His one and only 
Son, that whoever believes in 
Jesus shall not perish, but have
eternal life." "Anyone who calls
on the name of the Lord will 
be saved." What amazing grace
it is! Since God was aware 
that we couldn't go to heaven
by our own, He prepared 
the way that we can be 
saved only by faith in Jesus.
That's why we wouldn't make
any excuse saying, "I'm not
saved for lack of knowledge,
religious rituals and formalities."
Anyone can believe in Jesus.
Once we believe, we, through
faith in Christ, can go to heaven.
However, after we believe in
Jesus, we continue to worship,
serve and be faithful to Him,
because He prepares beautiful
houses for us with what we
do on this earth. The Lord
says, "I'll go there to prepare
a place for you." Why hasn't
He completed it? He going 
to prepare. If we go up to 
heaven, the places for us  
will be all different: some are
glorious and others ordinary.
What we work for the Lord
on earth will be sent up to 
heaven, with which the Lord 
will decorate our places beautiful.
If He finishes decorating, 
he'll call us one after another.   
When I held a crusade in 
Daejon, a pastor told me 
a testimony, "Pastor, there
was an elder in my church, 
who didn't cooperate with us
in building a new church. He 
had a lot of money but didn't 
offer any. We had to stop 
building it and had serious 
troubles. Then all of a sudden 
the elder died of a heart attack. 
While his sons and daughters 
were wailing before his dead 
body, he arose! Then he shouted 
at them, 'You must sell all my 
possessions and use them for 
building a church.' His sons and 
daughters were puzzled. Then 
he said to them, 'After I died 
I was guided by the angels to 
heaven, which was so beautiful. 
The road to heaven was located 
between stars. I walked along 
the road, looking at them. When 
I entered into heaven, the Lord 
welcomed me. He allowed me to 
see my house there, so I went 
to some place, where there were 
such wonderful houses. Among 
them I saw a house of an old 
widow who used to clean our 
church every morning. Her house 
was so great that I thought 
my house would be much greater 
than hers. But, as I was walking 
forward, houses by the road 
became smaller and smaller. 
I asked the angel, "We are going 
the wrong way, aren't we?
The house of the old lady who 
cleaned my church is so great.
Yet, the houses are getting smaller
and smaller. I was higher than 
her as an elder. what's wrong?
We must have taken a wrong way."
Then the angel said, "Just follow 
me." So they walked on and found 
a very little house at the corner.
The angel said to the elder, 
"That is your house." Then he
asked, "Why is my house so
very small?" The angel said,
"You were an elder in the world
and God blessed you so much 
that you were rich, but you did
nothing for God. Yet, the old 
widow devoted herself to God.
She got up early for the daybreak 
prayer meeting and made 
the church warm and cleaned it.
What she did was sent up 
and with that we have built 
a great house for her. But you
didn't do anything for God, so
you have to live at this little
house forever." Then the elder
held the angel's robe and said,
"If you send me back to the 
world, I'll build a wonderful
church and work a lot for God.
Please give me that opportunity."
He cried and pleaded with the
angel earnestly. Then the Lord
said, "You may go and you
have to build a church and 
accomplish what you promise."
So he woke up. At the time,
the name of the pastor of the
elder's church was Goh and
he said, "The elder awoke and
my church was rescued, because
he sold all his possessions and 
spent them for building a new
and beautiful church." For what 
we do on this earth for the Lord, 
He will reward us. He says,
"When I return, I will reimburse
you for any extra expense you
may have." Therefore, since our 
Lord is our way, we can go to 
heaven through Him. But after
we go there, the eternal glory
we will have will be up to what
we do on earth for the Lord.
People are wondering if there
will be heaven after they die.
The apostle, Paul, gave a clear
testimony about the heaven he
experienced in 2 Corinthians 5:1, 
"Now we know that if the earthly 
tent we live in is destroyed,
we have a building from God, 
an eternal house in heaven, not
built by human hands." He said
that if the earthly tents we live 
in are destroyed we have eternal
houses in heaven not built by
human hands. For the apostle 
experienced it. While preaching
the gospel in Lystra, He was 
stoned and killed by enemies.
They dragged and threw him
into the trash, around which 
his disciples prayed and he was
revived. Until that time, He 
had a tremendous experience
that he ascended to heaven.
In 2 Corinthians 12:1-4 we read, 
"I must go on boasting. 
Although there is nothing to be
gained, I will go on to visions
and revelations from the Lord.
I know a man in Christ who 
fourteen years ago was caught
up to the third heaven. whether
it was in the body or out of 
the body I do not know -- 
God knows. And I know that 
this man -- whether in the body
or apart from the body I do not
know, but God knows -- was
caught up to paradise. He heard
inexpressible things, things that
man is not permitted to tell."
When he was caught up to 
heaven, it was so clear that
he didn't know whether he was
in the body or out of the body.
He saw and heard inexpressible 
things, things that man couldn't 
describe. After he experienced
that, he always missed heaven
and confessed that he'd be far 
better to leave his body and 
be with the Lord. Philippians
1:21-23 says, "For to me, to 
live is Christ and to die is 
gain. If I am to go on living
in the body, this will mean
fruitful labor for me. Yet what
shall I choose? I do not know!
I am torn between the two; I
desire to depart and be with
Christ, which is better by far."
He said, "If I live on this earth 
it will be good because I can
do a lot of things for the Lord 
and help many people. If I left the 
world I would be with the Lord, 
so it would be much better.
I am torn between the two;
I desire to depart and be with
Christ, which is better by far."
The apostle, Paul, and we are 
so different, aren't we? People 
say that it will be better to be
in the world though they are 
in bad situations. That's because
they haven't seen any glory of
heaven. Peter was not sad even
if it would be near for him to 
leave his bodily tent but said 
with great expectation. We read
in 2 Peter 1:12-14, "So I will
always remind you of these
things, even though you know
them and are firmly established
in the truth you now have. I
think it is right to refresh your
memory as long as I live in 
the tent of this body, because
I know that I will soon put it
aside, as our Lord Jesus Christ
has made clear to me." Saying
that he would soon put aside 
the tent of his body, Peter plainly
witnessed that he would not 
live on this earth permanently.
When the apostle, John, was
banished to the island of Patmos,
impressed by the Holy Spirit 
while praying on the Lord's day,
he saw beautiful heaven. He said
in Revelation 21:1-7, "Then I
saw a new heaven and a new
earth, for the first heaven and 
the first earth had passed away,
and there was no longer any sea.
I saw the Holy City, the new
Jerusalem, coming down out 
of heaven from God, prepared 
as a bride beautifully dressed 
for her husband. And I heard 
a loud voice from the throne
saying, 'Now the dwelling of 
God is with men, and He will 
live with them. They will be 
His people, and God Himself
will be with them and be their
God. He will wipe every tear
from their eyes. There will be
no more death or mourning or
crying or pain, for the old order
of things has passed away.' He 
Who was seated on the throne 
said, 'I am making everything
new!' Then He said, 'Write this
down, for these words are 
trustworthy and true.' He said
to me: 'It is done. I am the Alpha
and the Omega, the Beginning 
and the End. To him who is 
thirsty I will give to drink 
without cost from the spring 
of the water of life. He who 
overcomes will inherit all this, 
and I will be his God and he
will be my son.' "  What a
beautiful scene the Lord showed
John on the island of Patmos!
He saw a new heaven and a
new earth, not this old earth,
which is filled with the curse,
the rumors of wars, earthquakes,
starvation, pains and sins.
The Lord burned the heavens and
earth with fire and built them
new. Moreover, there came down
the new Jerusalem from heaven.
It was floating in the universe
like the moon. It was so splendid
that it looked like a bride beautifully 
dressed for her husband. We
usually say that women are
beautiful. When are they most
beautiful? It is when they marry
because they are dressed most
gorgeously. I speak at the wedding
so often that I see beautiful and 
pretty brides. When I see them
at the wedding, all of them are
pretty. Dressed beautifully, they
look like an angel. When John
saw the new Jerusalem coming
down, it was so beautiful and
inexpressible that he said that
it was like a bride dressed
beautifully for her husband.
The new Jerusalem will be 
the house where Jesus and 
the resurrected saints will live
forever. The Lord built this new 
heaven and earth so beautifully 
that the Jews and Jacob's offsprings
will be prosperous there. If they
increase, they will move from
universe to universe. By the way,
Peter said that the day of the
Lord would come like a thief.
In 2 Peter 3:10-13 we read,
"But the day of the Lord will
come like a thief. The heavens
will disappear with a roar; the
elements will be destroyed by 
fire, and the earth and everything
in it will be laid bare. Since
everything will be destroyed in
this way, what kind of people 
ought you to be? You ought 
to live holy and godly lives 
as you look forward to the day
of God and speed its coming."
Look at the earthquake that 
occurred in Taiwan. While they
were sleeping, suddenly there 
came an earthquake so thousands
of people died and were injured
and their economy is in turmoil.
It is an irresistible force. When
a little earthquake occurs, the 
politics and economy of a nation
are destroyed. Still more, when
the heavens pass away and the
earth are melt by strong fire,
who will hide from the judgment
of God's wrath? No one will
survive. Therefore, we have to
wake up, accept Jesus as our 
Savior and live holy lives.
So we live as strangers and 
travelers on earth and when
the Lord comes we'll say, 
"Lord, here I am," and we 
will be caught up to heaven.
The place we will enter will
be the new Jerusalem. The place
that reigns over a new heaven 
and new earth will be the new
Jerusalem. There Jesus and His
brides will live together. They 
won't see the temple building, 
for Almighty God and His 
Little Lamb will be the temple.
At the place as beautiful as 
a bride dressed for her husband
God Himself will reign. Since 
God is the source of life, where
God dominates there will be 
no more tear, death, crying, 
mourning, nor pain. Then what
will be there instead? There will
be water of life overflowing and
we will take a bath with the 
water which will flow from the 
throne of God and His Little Lamb.
We will be overflowing with life
and there will be various kinds
of fruitful trees on both sides of 
life. If we ate the fruit, we will 
be overflowing with life, joy and 
hope. If its leaf is thrown to 
the people who live in the new
heaven and earth, it will become
the source of life which will
revive the whole nation. Likewise,
God will grant us lives overflowing
with life. Revelation 22:1-4 states,
"Then the angel showed me the 
river of the water of life, as clear
as crystal, flowing from the throne
of God and of the Lamb down 
the middle of the great street 
of the city. On each side of 
the river stood the tree of life,
bearing twelve crops of fruit,
yielding its fruit every month.
And the leaves of the tree are
for the healing of the nations. 
No longer will there be any 
curse. The throne of God and
of the Lamb will be in the city,
and His servants will serve Him.
They will see His face and His
name will be on their foreheads."
There will be no more night,
for the Lord, God, will be 
the light and we will reign 
with Him forever and ever.
Revelation 21:23 says, "The city
does not need the sun or the
moon to shine on it, for the
glory of God gives it light, 
and the Lamb is its lamp."
There will be no night, for
God and Jesus will be light.
So the lamp and the sun
will be useless. Therefore,
the Lord is our light and we
will reign with the Lord in life.
That means we will overflow
with life and care for and
reign over numerous chosen
people living on this earth. 
Our Shepherd, Jesus, will take
care of us with His good and
gentle heart and at last He 
will lead us to the house of
our Father so that we may 
live there forever. The place
will be truly overflowing with
truth all the time and there we 
will meet all the saints who 
died before and will rejoice, 
leaping and hugging them.
Those who don't believe in
the Lord weep when their
family members die, because 
they won't meet again. 
Yet, when we die, the Lord 
will take us and we will meet 
together at the heavenly gate.
Therefore, we are separate 
just awhile but never forever. 
The Bible says, "Brothers, we
do not want you to be ignorant
about those who fall asleep,
or to grieve like the rest of 
men, who have no hope. We
believe that Jesus died and 
rose again and so we believe
that God will bring with Jesus
those who have fallen asleep
in Him." Those who have no 
hope are those who don't 
believe in the Lord. They are
hopeless. They themselves have 
no hope nor hope that they 
will meet their family members 
who have fallen asleep. However, 
the Bible says that the Lord 
will bring with Him those 
who have fallen asleep in Him.
Therefore, we will be caught
up and the Lord will bring
those who have fallen asleep,
then we will say hallelujah 
and embrace one another, 
rejoicing altogether and live
with the Lord forever and ever.
Hence, brothers and sisters,
no matter how luxurious and 
beautiful our houses may be 
on this earth, we have to leave 
them in less than 100 years. 
No matter how beautiful and
young our bodies may look, 
they will be like broken tents
in less than 100 years. Yet, 
the eternal house that God 
prepared, not made by human 
hands, will wait for us. Therefore, 
all the people regardless rich and 
poor, will leave this earth sooner 
or later. On that day, you should 
not fall into hell, crying with 
eternal despair, frustration and 
sorrow, but may you exclaim 
hallelujah with praise and glory, 
enter into heaven and live with 
the Lord forever. Hallelujah!


Now, let us bow down and
pray together. Almighty God,
our Father. When we think of
the grace and love of our Lord,
we heartily thank You. You 
loved and called us from dirt 
and made us Your holy children 
so that we might cross over the 
bridge of Jesus passing through 
the valley of sin and death and 
enter into heaven. we are so
grateful to You that we can't 
express our thanks with our lips.   
As the apostle, Paul, rebuked
death saying, "Where, O death,
is your victory? Where, O death,
is your sting," we don't fear 
death, either. Our Father, God,
we thank You that we will enter 
into eternal heaven whenever 
death tears our bodily tents.
Dear Father, please let all those
who listen to today's sermon
never miss the way to eternal life
but get into the heaven that You
prepared. And while living on
this earth, please help them
work hard for the Lord so they 
may send up a lot of good 
materials to heaven, where 
beautiful houses may be built. 
We pray in Jesus' name. Amen.
