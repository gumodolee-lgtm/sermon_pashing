오늘의 말씀: 왜 기도해야 하나?
Why Should We Pray?
엡 6:18 - 19

Animals never pray though 
they face any crisis, for they
do not have any natural 
understanding about God in 
their souls. But people have
subconsciousness about God
in their souls by nature 
without learning. That's why
those who do not believe in
God seek and cry out to Him
when they are confronted with
some crises. Our knowledge 
about God is not made by our
thought. God revealed Himself
through the history of Israel 
and He sent His Son, Jesus, 
as a man 2000 years ago so 
that we could see, hear, touch 
and experience God in Jesus. 
When we believe in Jesus, 
we can accept God, and He 
becomes our Father and we
become His children. Therefore,
we must have a relationship
with God, our Father, by prayer.
First, why should we pray?
Because God wants our prayer
and He loves us. Does He 
need anything? He has love.
Love wants to talk and stay
with the one it loves without 
ceasing. You love your children,
don't you? It is natural that
you want to talk with your
children. Married couples want
to spent much time talking if
they love each other. We also
want to meet and talk every 
day with our friends we love.
That's why love wants to be
and talk with the one it loves.
Our God wants to stay and
talk with us because He loves
us. It will be done by prayer.
He will be greatly pleased 
with our worship and praise.
In the old testament days,
people brought offerings to
worship and meet God. 
Yet, today our sacrifice is 
to worship, praise and pray 
to God. The more we worship,
praise and pray, the more God
is with us. As God showed up
in Solomon's dream and blessed
him when he offered a sacrifice,
so when we keep worshipping,
praising and praying, God will
be much pleased with us and 
reveal Himself. Psalm 50:23 says,
"He who sacrifices thank offerings
honors me, and he prepares
the way so that I may show 
him the salvation of God."
When we continually worship,
praise, thank and honor God
by prayer, He will save us
with His righteous right hand.
Psalm 16:8-9 tells us, "I have
set the Lord always before me.
Because He is at my right hand,
I will not be shaken. Therefore
my heart is glad and my tongue
rejoices; my body also will rest
secure." How can we set the
Lord always before us and at
our right hand? That's because
we can enter the Most Holy 
Place through prayer and stay 
with God as His children.
When we have worldly hearts
without praying, we will be 
far away from God and live 
in the world. But if we change
our hearts and pray to God,
in an instant we can go into 
the Most Holy Place beyond
time and space and talk with
God and have a fellowship
with Him as His loved children.
That's why prayer can fulfill
God's wish. We should pray, 
for we can please and honor God.
After we please God, He must
pour joy and help upon us.
Secondly, we should pray,
because we can be filled with
the Holy Spirit by prayer. 
We live in body and continue 
to see, hear, speak, smell and
touch physical things in this 
world. Only when the Holy 
Spirit comes upon us, we can
get into heaven and have 
a relationship with God and 
Jesus Christ. The Holy Spirit
brings us the heavenly space
and time. That is, He brings
us the heavenly relationship.
That's why our Christian life
filled with the Holy Spirit is
filled with heaven. If we are
filled with the Holy Spirit,
heaven will be opened and
the kingdom of God will come 
into us. Though we live in body 
in this world, we can live in 
glorious heavenly grace with 
the fullness of the Holy Spirit.
When 120 believers eagerly 
prayed on the pentecost day, 
suddenly a sound like the blowing
of a violent wind came from 
heaven and what seemed to be
tongues of fire that separated
came upon each of them.
All of them were filled with
the Holy Spirit and began to
speak in other tongues as 
the Spirit enabled them and 
their souls were filled with 
heaven. All of them including
uneducated disciples ran out
and preached the gospel as soon
as they were filled with heaven.
They filled the whole world 
with heaven. Therefore, the Holy
Spirit comes upon us through
our prayer. Prayer is like a pipe
through which we can drink 
living water of the Holy Spirit.
John 7:37-38 reads, "On the last
and greatest day of the Feast, 
Jesus stood and said in a loud
voice, 'If anyone is thirsty, let
him come to me and drink.
whoever believes in me, as the 
Scripture has said, steams of 
living water will flow from
within him.' "  Likewise prayer
is like a pipe through which we
can drink living water in Christ.
No matter how much water 
the reservoir holds, people can't
use water to drink, wash and
take a bath unless they have
a pipe. They have to link a pipe.
Prayer is like a pipe linked to
Jesus so we can drink enough
living water of the Holy Spirit.
That's why the more we pray,
the more we can drink living
water and be filled with the
Holy Spirit. Therefore if the 
church doesn't pray, it becomes
dry and can't do any work
of life. Yet, if the church prays,
it will overflow with living water
and become the place bearing 
tremendous fruit like the garden 
of Eden and Elim. If we have
a relationship with the Holy Spirit
through prayer, we will be 
impressed. The Holy Spirit is 
not still in us. He makes us
repent of our sins and washes
away us from sins. He makes 
us see heaven and recognize
God and Jesus Christ so as to 
see eternal future. The Holy Spirit
makes us impressed so that we
may pray with thanks and tears
continually and He comforts us.
No matter how much we were
afflicted and hurt, if we pray,
the Holy Spirit comforts and 
strengthens us to overcome
all the trials. The Holy Spirit
also reveals the truth so that
we can realize the word sweetly,
deeply and gloriously and dwell
in heaven. The Holy Spirit guides
our lives. He holds our hands
and avoids dangers and leads
us into the safe place as our
shepherd and helps us sacrifice
our bodies, souls and spirits 
at the altar of God. In order
to experience these tremendous
things, we must pray. Without
praying, we can't be filled with
the Holy Spirit, have a relationship
with Him, nor experience heavenly
glory. All the heavenly truth is
a theory. But when we experience
it, that becomes ours. We can
experience the truth of God 
through the Holy Spirit and 
then it becomes ours.
Thirdly, we should pray, for
the demon will be driven out
in the fire of prayer. 
Since Adam and Eve fell, the 
devil and demon have occupied
this world, stolen, killed and
destroyed people. However, 
when we pray, the devil will
flee in the name of Jesus Christ
and the power of His blood.
When we pray, the blood of
Jesus will work. Acts 8:6-8
says, "When the crowds heard
Philip and saw the miraculous
signs he did, they all paid close
attention to what he said. With
shrieks, evil spirits came out 
of many, and many paralytics
and cripples were healed. So
there was great joy in that city."
The demon makes people dirty
and ugly, but if he is driven 
out in the name of Jesus Christ
and the power of His blood,
they will become new.
The demon becomes the cause
of spiritual, mental and physical
pollution. Today we say many
kinds of environmental pollution,
but the pollution of our character
comes seriously due to the demon.
If the dirty demon enters us,
our character will be polluted.
We will become wicked if
the evil spirit comes into us.
If the lying demon comes into
us, we will be polluted as 
a liar. If the fortune telling
demon enter us, we will be
polluted as a fortune teller.
If the worldly demon comes
into us, we will follow all
sorts of the worldly things
and won't be interested in
heaven at all. If we are caught
by the demon of oppression, 
we will be stressed and have
many kinds of sicknesses 
mentally and physically and
become frustrated and disappointed.
Acts 10:38 tells us, "How God
anointed Jesus of Nazareth with
the Holy Spirit and power, and
how He went around doing 
good and healing all who were
under the power of the devil,
because God was with Him."
If we pray and repent, Jesus'
blood and the Holy Spirit work
and we will be freed from
the demon and have liberty.
Therefore, if we praise and
prepare the Lord, our hearts
will be refreshed, clean and
be filled with joy, for the dirty
and wicked demon and many
types of demons are driven out.
Then the Holy Spirit comes into 
us and helps us bear beautiful 
fruit: love, joy, peace, patience, 
kindness, goodness, faithfulness, 
gentleness, and self-control. 
Fourth, only prayer moves
God's hand. Though God, who
made heaven, earth, the world 
and everything in it, wants to
work for us, He can work only
when we pray. We can see the 
instruction in the old testament.
Psalm 91:15 states, "He will call
upon me, and I will answer him;
I will be with him in trouble,
I will deliver him and honor him."
Jeremiah 33:3 also says, "Call to 
me and I will answer you and 
tell you great and unsearchable
things you do not know."
We can see the instruction
in the new testament. 
In Luke 18:1, "Jesus told His
disciples that they should 
always pray and not give up."
James 4:1-2 says, "What causes
fights and quarrels among you?
Don't they come from your 
desires that battle within you?
You want something but don't
get it. You kill and covet, but
you cannot have what you want. 
You quarrel and fight. You do
not have, because you do not
ask God."  We do not get,
because we do not pray.
As a result, we quarrel and fight.
Let us see the life of Moses.
When the Israelites were coming
out of Egypt, God worked after
Moses prayed. The Israelites were
at the Red Sea and there was
no place for them to go. The
Egyptian army ran up to capture
them and the Red Sea blocked
the way. All the Israelites cried,
grumbled and grieved. Then 
Moses prayed and God worked.
In Exodus 14:15-16 the Lord said 
to Moses, "Why are you crying 
out to me? Tell the Israelites
to move on. Raise your staff
and stretch out your hand over
the sea to divide the water 
so that the Israelites can go 
through the sea on dry ground."
When Moses prayed, God commanded
him to divide the Red Sea.
If Moses had not prayed,
God would not have worked.
And also bitter water at Marah
became sweet. In Exodus 15:25,
"Moses cried out to the Lord, 
and the Lord showed him 
a piece of wood. He threw it
into the water, and the water
became sweet." When Moses' 
hand moved, God worked.
There was a famous prayer,
Pastor Jorge Muller, in England.
In fact, he was a German and
went to England to study English
to be a missionary. There he
saw too many orphans wandering
and suffering on the street.
One day while he was reading 
the Bible saying, "God is 
the father of orphans and 
the defender for widows,"
the Holy Spirit told him in
his heart, "God is the father
of orphans so you should care
for orphans." Then he gave up
being a missionary. After he 
prayed and decided to take care 
of orphans and established 
an orphanage in Ashily Town.
He cared for 2000 orphans by
feeding and educating them 
and helped them get married.  
He devoted his whole life to 
orphans, yet he didn't beg any
private or public organization,
or any church for money.
Only with a Bible, he prayed
at the altar and cared for 2000
orphans. By the way, once on a
cold winter day, a heater didn't
work at the orphanage for 
abandoned kids and babies.
They had to turn it off for a
week to fix but then the kids 
might die on such a terribly
cold day in England. It was
urgent. People said, "Pastor,
kids and babies may die,
because the broken heater."
Yet, Pastor Muller was not
puzzled at all and went to
church with a Bible. He knelt
down before God and prayed
during the night, "Lord, who
controls weather and temperature!
You gave me these little kids.
You are their Father and I'm
just in charge of them. I'm 
sure you never let your kids
die. Though it is winter, please
change the weather like spring
for a week!" It is impossible
to man, but Lord who controls
time, please change this winter
to spring!" Then before they 
turn off the heater to fix, all 
of a sudden the weather was
turned to spring all over the
country! It was so warm that
people changed the winter
clothes to the spring clothes.
They didn't understand the
strange weather. There was
no El Nino at the time, but
God made the whole England
warm. Therefore, they didn't
need the heater for the kids
for a week. Mechanics repaired
the heater so hard. They said,
"Pastor, the heater works well
now." As soon as they turned
on the heater, warm weather
disappeared and a cold day
began again. All the people
said to one another, "What's
matter? For a week it was
spring and why again winter?"
They put on winter clothes 
again. Our God performs 
unimaginable miracles when 
we depend on Him. But He
works only when we pray.
Fifth. we should pray, for prayer 
brings God's grace, blessing and 
help to our neighbors through us.
We must pray that God may
help our neighbor countries.
That's why we believers should
not neglect praying for our family.
Acts 10:2 says about Cornelius,
"He and all his family were
devout and God-fearing; he 
gave generously to those in 
need and prayed to God regularly."
In Acts 16:31 reads, "Believe 
in the Lord Jesus, and you 
will be saved -- you and 
your household." If we believe
in Jesus, God wants all our
family to be saved. Therefore,
if children are saved, they should
pray that their parents and
brothers and sisters may be
saved; husbands for their wives
and children; wives for 
their husbands and children.
In 2 Corinthians 9:8 "God is
able to make all grace abound
to you, so that in all things 
at all times, having all that 
you need, you will abound in 
every good work." Since God 
wants to bless our business 
and life abundantly, we should
pray to fulfill His wish. 
We have to continue to pray
for our business and life. We 
should also pray for our neighbor 
and nation without ceasing.
What we can pray is our 
privilege. When we pray, 
God's hand begins to move.
Unless we pray, God has no
condition to work among us.
If God wants to work without
our prayer, the devil will 
slander us, "Lord, why do you
bless those who don't pray?
It is not right." Therefore, 
unless we pray, God can't work 
for us. Yet, when we pray, 
God is willing to help us due
to our prayer. The power of
His right hand will be shown.
That's why the devil tells us,
"Do not pray. You may study
the Bible and go to church but
don't pray. If you pray, you
are mad and a zealot. Do not
pray." Because the devil becomes
powerless and has to be driven
out if we pray, he strongly tries
to let us down so we can't pray.
Today churches are corrupted,
for they do not pray. They 
become cold without praying
and God doesn't reveal His power.
Since they don't experience His
power, the heaven is just theory
and they don't experience it and
all the believers become wrong.
We must pray continually. 
Deuteronomy 28:7-8 states, 
"The Lord will grant that 
the enemies who rise up against
you will be defeated before you.
They will come at you from one
direction but flee from you in
seven. The Lord will send a
blessing on your barns and on
everything you put your hand to.
The Lord your God will bless
you in the land He is giving you."
God wants to do these ones, 
only when we pray. Unless we 
pray, He won't do those things.
Colossians 4:2-3 says, "Devote
yourselves to prayer, being 
watchful and thankful. And pray
for us, too, that God may open
a door for our message, so that
we may proclaim the mystery 
of Christ."  Paul pleaded with 
us to pray. Therefore we should
pray for ministers, elders, senior
deacons and deaconesses, district
and home cell leaders, Sunday
school teachers and all the
people who work for the Lord,
so that the grace of God may
come upon them. Prayer brings
joy and peace but also heavy 
burden to fight the devil. We
must make effort and try to
pray. We can't pray automatically
without doing anything. 
We have to cooperate with
each other that we may pray.
Matthew 18:18-19, "I tell you
the truth, whatever you bind on 
earth will be bound in heaven,
and whatever you loose on earth
will be loosed in heaven. Again,
I tell you that if two of you
on earth agree about anything
you ask for, it will be done for
you by my Father in heaven."
The Lord said, "Where there 
are two or three in my name, 
I will be in the midst of them."  
Therefore, my beloved saints,
prayer is the privilege God has
given us, the key to know 
heaven, the power to defeat 
the devil, and the authority 
to move God's hand. 
May you always 
be alert and pray!
