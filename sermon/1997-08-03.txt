

The Love and the Commandment


If we truly love God and feel
and are confident of His warm 
love, how pleased we would be!
The Bible says, "There is 
no fear in love.  But perfect 
love drives out fear."
If there is only safety 
without fear in our lives, 
where can we have 
more happiness than this?
How can we keep this 
relationship of love with God?
How can we truly love God
and sense and experience
and are sure of His love?
First, we will think about 
the relation between love and 
commandment.  When God 
made man and put him in Eden, 
His love was indescribable.
In the Bible, in the cool of 
the day, God came to Eden
and took the hands of Adam 
and Eve to Eden and talked
with them face to face.
How amazing it is!
If we heard the voice of God
just once, we would be excited

and our lives would be changed.
Adam and Eve had a face 
to face fellowship with God.
Then how happy and joyful
they were!
However the love of God and 
the fellowship with Him are on 
the basis of the command of God.
"You are free to eat from any 
tree in the garden; but you 
must not eat from the tree of 
the knowledge of good and 
evil, for when you eat of it 
you will surely die."
This is the commandment
to keep love between God 
and Adam and Eve.
When this command is observed
God can have fellowship of love 
with Adam and Eve.  If this 
command is neglected the relationship 
with God will be broken. 
Yet, Adam disregarded 
the relationship of love with God.  
He thought he could love God 
and be love by Him without 
keeping the commandment.
He accepted the temptation of
devil and took the fruit of good 
and evil which God commanded 
him not to eat.  Then the Glory
of God suddenly disappeared. 
When the glory of God went 
away, Adam and Eve felt 
they were bare and hid behind 
the tree because of fear.  Fear 
entered into them instead of love.
As a result, their relationship
with God was broken and 
they were driven out of Eden.
Instead of love, the earth was
cursed and produced thorns and
thistles, and their bodies were 
to be sick and die. 
The relationship between God 
and Adam and Eve is only when 
they observe the command.
If we neglect the condition, love 
will go away. All the blessings 
of God require condition. When we 
keep the condition we will be blessed. 
When we violate the condition 
the blessings will disappear.
Secondly, let us see about Jesus
Christ and the lesson of love.
Jesus said in John 15: 10,
"If you obey My commands, 
you will remain in My love, 
just as I have obeyed My 
Father's commands and 
remain in His love."
When the Son of God Jesus
came in flesh, He was loved 
by God and He loved God.
His relationship of love with God 

had the condition of command.
He said, "Just as I obey My 
Father's command and remain
in His love, you will remain in 
me if you obey My command."
The command God has given 
Jesus is absolute obedience 
and the death on the cross.
Absolute obedience to the word
of God and the death on the cross
are the command God gave Jesus.
When Jesus obeyed the word 
of God and died on the cross,
He could have everlasting talk
of love and relationship with God.
If Jesus had not obeyed the 
command nor died on the cross,
the love of God would have
left Him and the fellowship with 
God would have been broken.
That's why Jesus Christ said,
"I obey the command of God
and I remain in His love."
Jesus made up His mind
for that every day.
While He was ready to obey 
God and die on the cross, 
He was overflowing 
with the love God.
As a result, Jesus died on the
cross in place of the mankind.
"I have done everything." means 
"I have obeyed God's command."
That's why God loves Jesus
Christ so much. The command 
is the condition of love.  God 
showed such a great love to Jesus.
Therefore, today we are loved 
by God, and we must observe

His command to love Him.
For we should testify our love
if we say we love God.
The evidence of our love toward 
Jesus is shown in John 14:15,

please repeat after me,
"If you love Me, you will 
obey what I command."
Therefore we should obey
the command as the condition
of love toward Jesus and God.
When we obey the command,
what we say, "we love God,
we love Jesus" is true.
It is not a lie.  But if we
don't obey the command and
say we love God and Jesus,
it is a lie.  I John 5:3-4 reads, 
"This is love for God: to obey 
His commands. And His commands 
are not burdensome, for everyone 
born of God overcomes the world.
This is the victory that has over-
come the world, even our faith."
If we are saved only when
we observe Ten Commandments,
it will be burdensome.
We can't keep the command.
However, today since we are 
saved by believing in Jesus 
freely without any condition,
we should obey the command
to have a fellowship with God
and to love Him continually.
Therefore, after we are saved
the command is not hard for us.
The command was a fearful 
master, but it changed to 
a servant to help us:
a servant to make us love 
God and have a relationship 
with Him continually.
The commandment of the old
testament was to kill people,
but that of the new testament
is to help, rescue, and lead us
as a servant.  Thirdly, let us 
check each commandment
why we can survive only 
when we obey the command.
Why do people and the command
have such a deep relation?
The Ten Commandments of
the Lord are different from
all the other words of God.
Other words of God were written
by the inspiration of prophets,
kings, and priests, but Ten command-
ments were directly written 
by God at Sinai Mountain. 
What God wrote directly was
only Ten Commandments.
The others are spoken to us
through man by the Holy Spirit.
We can see the command is
very important to God.
The command God wrote by 
His hands must not be neglected. 
The Bible says, "Do not have
other gods before me."
Compare this command with 
love.  If we love God, 
we should love God only, 
but if we worship other gods 
and say we love God, 
is the love true?
If a man says to his wife,
"I love you," and loves another
woman, that love is not true.
Therefore, if we worship another
god, our love for God is false,
and the love is disregarded.
"You shall not bow down
to idol gods."  God detests
idol gods so much. 
The Israelites were abandoned, 
for they worshipped and bow 
down to idol gods like Baal 
and Asherah. North Israel and
South Juda were abandoned

because they worshipped idols.
Since Solomon worshipped idols,
the nation was divided.
If we worship idols God hates,
we are against Him, aren't we?
After we do what God detests,
if we say, "Lord, I love you"

then will He accept our word?
If we love God, we should 
hate what He hates.  
Idols can't be with 
the love of God.  
"You shall not misuse the name 
of the Lord your God."
If you love God but you 
misuse His name, does it 
mean you truly love Him?
We usually honor the one
we love, but if we misuse 
his name, we insult him.
It is against love, isn't it?
Then we can't say we love God.

"Keep the Sabbath day holy."
We don't keep Saturday as
Sabbath day, but Sunday.
For our Lord said, "I am
the Lord" and He came to
meet His saints and poured out
the Holy Spirit on Sunday.
Jesus always came to see
His disciples on Sunday.
Today Jesus says that we 
should work for six days 
and come to meet Him
on the seventh day.
The Lord comes to the church
and is waiting for us.
That's why we should worship 
and meet Him at the church.
If we keep the Lord waiting
for us and leave Him alone
for our private matter, what
we say, "we love the Lord" 
is a lie.  "Honor your 
father and your mother."
Parents are like the image
of God on the earth.
We call our earthly father and 
heavenly Father as our father.
If we don't honor our earthly 
father how can we say we 
truly honor our heavenly Father?
So God really wants us 
to honor our earthly parents.
There is no reward for other
commands, but His reward
for the honor of our parents.
"Honor your parents and you
will live long on the earth."
God wants us to honor our
parents promising long life,
but if we don't, we disregard 
Him, and we don't love Him.
"You shall not murder."
If we kill man made in the
image of God, can we say
we love God?  The Lord says
if we hate our brother in mind
it is like we murder him.
Therefore, the hatred deprives
us of the love of God.
When we repent of hatred and
drive it out of mind, we love God.
The Bible says, "You shall 
not steal."  If we steal, we 
can't love God.  For He is
always just, but not false.
If we steal, we completely deceive 
Him and trample His justice.
If we give a false testimony
against our neighbor and slander
them, God will not be pleased.
We should pray for our neighbors
and encourage them. 
Then God will bless us.
When we say we love God,
He will be happy.  Why?
Because He sent His only Son
as a ransom to save us.
If we bother and slander 
those for whom Jesus died, 
we resist God and His love 
can't dwell within us.
The Bible says, "You shall
not covet your neighbor."
We must not covet our 
neighbor's nor try to take it.
The Bible says, "You shall 
not covet your neighbor's
wife, man-servant, maid-servant,
donkeys, cows, and goats."
If we make effort and enjoy
our richness from God and 
give thanks to Him, He will
bless and love us more.
Yet, if we don't strive but
covet our neighbor and try to 
take his, it is greed, which
is idolatry. God punishes it.
Because of greed, Adam and 
Eve disobeyed God's command 
and were abandoned.
Therefore we should keep God's
command in mind and obey it,
then His grace will be great 
and it will be our testimony,
and the love of God will be
poured on us.  If we look at
our hearts and churches,
we realize that we have faith
and hope, but we lack love
for God and our neighbor.
God has poured His love on
us, but we don't experience 
the love.  That's because we
disobeyed His command in our lives.
If we obey His command and
are confident of our love for God
and His love for us, we can
be proud of our lives without
fear, for the Son of God has
come to us and dwells in us.
That's why we don't fear
nor are dismayed. 
"If God is for us, 
who can be against us?
He who did not spare His 
own Son, but gave Him up 
for us all --- how will He 
not also, along with Him, 
graciously give us all things?"
Like this, we can live with
confidence.  We are saved
freely by believing in Jesus,
but we can enjoy the love
and fellowship with God
only by obeying His command.
Love is overflowing with us only 
on the condition of the command.
