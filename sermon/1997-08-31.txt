Faithful Servant
Luke 19:11-27


The Lord is looking for the one
in the world, who is faithful to God.
In today's scripture, a parable
says, "A man of noble birth
went to a distant country 
to have himself appointed king 
and to return. So he called ten of
his servants and gave them ten
minas.  Later he was made king
and returned home.  He sent for
the servants to find out what 
they gained with the money.
The first one said, 'Sir, 
your mina has earned ten more.'
His master replied, 'Well done, 
my good servant!  Because you
have been trustworthy in a very
small matter, take charge of ten cities.'
The second one earned five more
with one mina.
His master said, "Well done,
you take charge of five cities.'
To the one who kept one mina
in cloth, the master said, 
'Throw out the wicked servant!
and take his mina away from him
and give it to the one 
who has ten minas.'
He said, "To everyone who has,
more will be given but as for 
the one who has nothing, even 
what he has will be taken away."
In this parable, the Lord tells us
that the one who is faithful
to what the lord gave He will 
bless, reward and more grace.
The most basic reason Adam 
and Eve fell in the garden of Eden 
was that they had no loyalty.
The fruit of good and evil 
is to test their loyalty.  
Adam threw away loyalty 
for his greed.  As a result, 
he was abandoned.
Even today God's most interest 
in us is loyalty.  He is searching 
us if we are faithful to Him or not
First, let us see about Job's loyalty.
The scripture says, "Job was 
loved by God very much.
He was blameless and upright;
he feared God and shunned evil."
One day there came the angels
and Satan before God.
There Satan slandered Job.
God said to Satan, "Have you 
considered Job?  He is blameless
and upright, a man who fears 
God and shuns evil."
Then Satan replied, "Does Job
fear God for nothing?  Have you
not put a hedge around him and 
his household and everything 
he has? Since You have blessed 
the work of his hands, he 
worships and is faithful to You."
But stretch out Your hand 
and strike everything he has, 
and he will surely curse You 
to Your face."
Like this, Satan slandered Job,
and said that he worshipped God
for his greed.  So God put Job
on Satan's hands to show how
faithful Job was.  Satan stroke
Job.  He thought that Job would
turn away from God if he deprived 
Job of his possessions and children.
Job had seven sons and three 
daughters, and he owned seven
thousand sheep, three thousand 
camels, five hundred yoke of oxen
five hundred donkeys, and had
a large number of servants.
Satan took all those livestock
in a day. Job's sons and daughter
were killed by a mighty wind
while they were feasting at
Job's oldest brother's house.
Job might have cursed God
in that situation, but he didn't.
He shaved his head and tore his
robe. Then he fell to the ground
in worship and said: 
"Naked I came from my mother's 
womb, and naked I will depart.
The Lord gave and the Lord
has taken away; 
may the name of the Lord 
be praised."
In all this, Job did not sin by 
charging God with wrongdoing,
and he wouldn't exchange his
loyalty for his possessions.
Then Satan said to the Lord,
"Job will curse if he loses
his health."  God accepted it
and Satan afflicted Job with
painful sores from the soles of 
his feet to the top of his head.
He couldn't wear his robe, for
his skin was torn and pus was
running out.  He scraped himself
with a piece of broken pottery
as he sat among the ashes.
Though he was suffering 
severely from sickness, 
he did not resent God, 
but thanked and praised Him
by keeping his faith in Him.
Then Satan entered into his wife,
who was closest and shared
trials and troubles with him,
and made her slander him.
She said to Job, "Are you still
holding on to your integrity?
Curse God and die!"
And she left him, and it made Job 
greatly shocked. Yet he replied,
"You are talking like a foolish 
woman.  Shall we accept good
from God, and not trouble?"
Then Job's three friends came
to comfort him, but Satan made
them offend him severely,
"You are judged by God, for you
sinned greatly against Him."
Yet Job said, "I've never sinned.
I don't know why I'm suffering
like this though I didn't sin 
in my conscience and work."
However, his friends blamed
him by turns saying, 
"You are suffering because 
you sinned greatly against God.  
Unless you repent of 
your sin and are humble, 
you will be abandoned.
They were going to discourage
Job and let him down, 
but he never doubted 
nor had a grudge against God.
In Job 23: 10-12, he said, 
"He knows the way that I take;
when He has tested me, I will 
come forth as gold.  My feet 
have closely followed His steps;
I have kept to His way without
turning aside. I have not departed
from the commands of His lips;
I have treasured the words of His 
mouth more than my daily bread."
In all these trials, Job didn't change 
his faith in God.  That's why God 
was greatly pleased with Job and 
blessed him twice as much.
God was greatly glorified through
Job and Satan disgraced himself
and turned out to be the father
of lie.  Likewise, through Job
God proved that man should 
worship God not because of 
blessing, but even when he 
was taken everything.
Secondly, let us see the faith and 
test of Jesus in the New Testament.
God even tested the faith of His 
Son Jesus before the ministry.
Jesus is the true God, but He 
came in a body of man.
He was baptized in Jordan River
at 30 and was taken to 
the wilderness by the Spirit of 
God and tempted by the devil.  
Jesus was not tempted by man, 
but the Holy Spirit led Him
to the wilderness and allowed
Him to be tempted.
Jesus fasted and prayed for
40 days and nights, so He
was very hungry, then the devil
came and tempted Him.
The Bible says in Matthew 4:3-4,
"The tempter came to Him and 
said, 'If you are the Son of God,
tell these stones to become bread.'
Jesus answered, 'It is written:
'Man does not live on bread 
alone, but on every word that 
comes from the mouth of God.' "
He was very hungry, and He
had all powers with which He
could do anything.  Therefore,
Satan said by exploiting that,
"Aren't you hungry? You can't
do anything in hunger.
You must first fill your hunger
with God's authority and power.
You are dying of hunger!
Where is God? Fill your hunger!
When Satan tempted Jesus,
He strictly said, "Man does not
live on bread alone, but on every
word that comes from the mouth 
of God."  We should not serve 
God after we fill our hunger,
but we should first keep His 
word though we are hungry.
Jesus never moved His heart
of faith in God.  
Then the devil told Jesus 
to exchange His faith for fame.
In Matthew 4:5-7, "Then the devil
took Him to the holy city and had
Him stand on the highest point of
the temple. 'If you are the Son
of God, he said, 'throw yourself 
down. For it is written: 'He will
command His angels concerning 
you, and they will lift you up in 
their hands, so that you will not 
strike your foot against a stone.'
Jesus answered him, 'It is also
written: 'Do not put the Lord 
your God to the test.'
At the temple of Jerusalem,
there were many delegates
to worship God.  Satan said to 
Jesus, "Didn't you come to have 
fame and to be the Lord? 
Why don't you throw yourself
down?  Aren't the angels 
of God your servants?  
Your angels will come to lift 
you up, so that you will not 
strike your foot against a stone, 
then those who saw you will 
worship, praise and follow you.
Therefore, exploit your power
for fame.  Why don't you use
the authority of God for fame."
Satan means that Jesus should
throw away loyalty for fame and 
use the power of God for greed.  
Then Jesus sternly said
that we must not test God.
Jesus wouldn't use the authority
of God for His own splendor.
He wouldn't misuse the authority
of God for fame. Then the devil
compared worldly power with faith,
"You'd better have worldly power,
so turn away from your faith
and take the worldly power."
Matthew 4:8-11 reads, "Again,
the devil took Him to a very
high mountain and showed Him
all the kingdoms of the world 
and their splendor. 'All this I
will give you,' he said, 'if you
will bow down and worship me.'
Jesus said to Him, 
'Away from Me, Satan!  
For it is written:
'Worship the Lord your God,
and serve Him only.'  Then 
the devil left Him, and angels
came and attended Him."
The devil said, "Put aside the faith 
in God and take the power by 
exploiting the authority and glory
of God." Then Jesus strictly said,
"I must worship God only, and
won't serve Satan for power."
Although the devil tried to tempt
Jesus, he couldn't take His faith.
He left Jesus, and angels came 
and served Him.
Like this, God even tested the faith
of His Son.  Jesus kept His faith
in God even at the moment of
His severe suffering on the cross.
In Philippians 2: 5-11 we reads,
"Your attitude should be the same
as that of Christ Jesus. Who, being
in very nature of God,. did not 
consider equality with God 
something to be grasped, but
made Himself nothing, taking 
the very nature of a servant,
being made in human likeness.
And being found in appearance
as a man, He humbled Himself
and became obedient to death
--- even death on a cross!
Therefore God exalted Him to
the highest place and gave Him
the name that is above every name,
that at the name of Jesus every
knee should bow, in heaven and 
on earth and under the earth,
and every tongue confess that
Jesus Christ is the Lord, to 
the glory of God the Father."
Jesus didn't die for nothing,
but the death on the cross
was the most shameful and
capital punishment.
In front of His disciples and
enemies, He was stripped of 
His robes. The One called
the Son of God was powerlessly
crucified and died, yet He didn't
ask for His life through 12 angels.
Since He was aware that His 
death on the cross was the will of 
God, He was faithful even to death. 
That's why God was greatly 
pleased with Jesus and exalted 
Him and gave Him the nam 
that is above every name, that
at Jesus every knee should bow. 
Therefore, what God is looking for
is faith in our hearts which is
what He wants most.
Thirdly, in order to be faithful,
what kind of ingredients should
we have in our hearts?

What is loyalty?
We can be faithful to God when 
we have fervent love Him.

Then why do we love God?
Because He created and 
redeemed us, we can love Him.
He has begotten us, hasn't He?
To save us, He sent His Son
and paid the penalty for our sin
through His death on the cross.
Since we experienced this great
love of God, we can love Him.
We didn't love God first, 
but He loved us first.
He is still our shepherd, and
the Holy Spirit is with us and 
guides us. Moreover God 
guarantees our future.
Though we leave our bodies,
He has prepared places for us
in heaven. The Lord has prepared
beautiful places where there will 
be no more crying, anxiety, 
parting, mourning, or sickness,
and He is waiting for us.
That's why we should have
fervent love for God so that
we can be faithful to Him.
Psalm 100: 3-4 says, "Know
that the Lord is God.  It is He
who made us, and we are His;
we His people, the sheep of His
pasture.  Enter His gates with
thanksgiving and His courts 
with praise; give thanks to 
Him and praise His name."
We can be faithful to God
if we respect and fear Him.
Those who misuse the name 
of God without fear and 
disregard Him will never 
be faithful to Him. 
We should fear the name 
of God and worship Him.
Why should we worship God?
Why should we fear Him?

For God is Creator. 
The universe and everything 
in it were created by His hands.  
Nothing was not made by Him. 
That's why God who created 
everything has absolute power 
and must be worshipped. 
He is sovereign Lord who 
rules over every creature.
Our God controls our lives,
country, nation, and history.
We must worship this sovereign
God. Furthermore, the elements
of loyalty include absolute faith
obedience.  He who is faithful 
to God should admire His will
and devote himself to Him.
In the parable, the one who 
received one mina made ten 
minas, and another one who 
received made five minas. 
They worked faithfully for things
that their master gave them.
We should serve God 
with sincerity in our lives.
We should keep the Sabbath
Day and worship God by giving
our tithe, and we should serve Him 
with our body, time and materials. 
We should honor God with heart, 
will, earnestness and life in complete 
faith and obedience. These are 
the essential ingredients for faith.
Without belief and obedience,
how can we be faithful to God?
Therefore, we should completely
obey God's commands with trust.
Another ingredient for faith is 
to be beyond our own interest.
The devil tested Job's faith 
with his interest, and the same
to Jesus.  Satan always tempts
us with our interest, 
"If you serve God, 
you will lose a lot.
If you go to church and give 
your tithe, you will lose much.
In this way, Satan intends to 
move our faith with our interest.
If we leave our loyalty and love
for God because of our good,
it is very little faith.
It is unworthy faith.
When we serve God, we should 
not change our faith, though we 
lose something and face distress 
and death.  You know 
the patriots including Ahn Jung 
Gun gave up all his youth and
his future, suffering and loved
the country and nation.
Wasn't he faithful to the nation?
Galatians 2: 20 says, "I have been
crucified with Christ and 
I no longer live, but Christ 
lives in me. The life I live 
in the body, I live by faith 
in the Son of God, who loved 
me and gave Himself for me."
Therefore, we should be with
Jesus and must be faithful to
Him though we lose anything.
Shadrach, Meshach and Abednego
though they were born in Babylon, 
they didn't bow down to the idol 
by disobeying the king's command 
without considering their positions 
and fame. When the king forced 
them to throw them into fiery 
furnace unless they bow down 
to his god, they said, 
"O king, if you do, our God 
will deliver us, and if not, 
we won't bow down to your 
god nor worship him." They 
gave up their lives for faith. 
That's why when they were 
thrown into fire, the fourth man, 
the Son of God Jesus, came 
and danced with them, and 
the fire didn't harm them at all.
When we give up our good,
we can have true faith.
We can also be faithful to God,
when we give all the glory
to God and are humble
without boasting ourselves.
After we work just a little,
we may boast what we did.

Then there is no glory.
To the one who boast all the
trial and effort will be in vain.
If we do anything for the Lord, 
we should make it in secret. 
Luke 17: 7-10 says, "Suppose
one of you had a servant plowing
or looking after the sheep.
Would he say to the servant
when he comes in from the field.
'Come along now and sit down
to eat'?  Would he not rather
say, 'Prepare my support, get
yourself ready and wait on me
while I eat and drink; after that
you may eat and drink'?
Would he thank the servant
because he did what he was 
told to do?  So you also, when 
you have done everything you 
were told to do, should say, 
'We are unworthy servants; 
we have only done our duty.' "
After we finish our work,
we have nothing to be praised
and to boast, for we have 
done what we have to do.
"I am an unworthy servant."
Jesus said like this with humility.
That is the true faith, but if
we do something and try to
boast about it, it won't bring
any faith and will be in vain.
What God asks us for basically.
is faith. Unless He tests our faith,
He will never leave us any
important thing nor bless us.
There is no more important
and blessing thing than to be
a faithful servant before God.
"Well done, my good servant!
Because you have been 
trustworthy in a very small 
matter, take charge of ten cities."
How wonderful this word is!
Therefore, if we have believed
in Jesus and become His children,
we should love and be faithful
to God with heart, will, mind and
life to please Him. Then though
Satan came from a direction,
he will flee away in seven
directions so that God may
bless you and you may take
charge of ten cities!
