
True Release and Freedom


Korea was forced to sign an 
annexation treaty by Japan in 1910.
Then, Japan made Korea
their slaves by colonial policy.
They forced us to change our
names in Japanese.  Even I had 
to use Yoshida Yoyokki instead 
of Cho Yonggi to enter school.
In that way, they intended to 
blot out everything on Korea.
They took away our language
and character, so we had to use
Japanese language and character.
If we used Korean language and 
character, we were caught, flogged, 
and punished. In this way, they 
completely subordinated Korea's 
history and culture to Japan.
After that, they compulsorily
conscripted Korean youth and
took Korean girls as comfort
girls for Japanese soldiers.
Korean adults were taken 
to coal mines and factories.
Yet, we couldn't say 
anything about that.  
Moreover, all the Koreans had 
to bow down to Japanese god. 
All these insults, shames, and 
contempts were indescribable.
However, on Aug 15 in 1945, 
God granted us freedom and 
liberty through the victory of
allied forces including America.
We were too deeply impressed to 
express by words.  First, we 
took back our names and freely 
spoke and learn our language.
On Aug 15 in 1948, we formed
our own government and our 
national flag flapped in the wind.
Without experiencing shackle and 
restraint, we can't understand the 
real meaning of release and freedom.
Yet, even if we experience 
freedom and liberty in our 
circumstances, we can't say 
it is true liberty and freedom.
Unless we have religions freedom
from bondage of the devil and sin,

we can't have release and liberty.
Man was the descendant of Adam,
who became a slave because of 
his fall.  Then we are born 
slaves to the devil and sin.
We can never get out of 
this slavery by ourselves.
That's why Jesus, the Son of God,
came 2,000 years ago and suffered 
on the cross because of our sin, 
unrighteousness, filth, curse, 
sickness, and death to give us 
complete release and freedom.
Through suffering, He wanted to 
give us true freedom and liberty.
The Lord proclaimed what His
true release and freedom were
to all creatures at the synagogue 
of Nazareth.  
First, the Lord said,
"I came to give the poor 
the good news so that they may 
have true release and freedom."
Originally man was not made
for poverty. In Genesis 1: 26-29,
we read, "God said, 'Let us 
make man in our image, 
in our likeness, and let them 
rule over the fish of the sea 
and the birds of the air, over 
the livestock, over all the earth, 
and over all the creatures 
that move along the ground.'
So God created man in His 
own image, in the image of God 
He created him; male and female 
He created them.  God blessed 
them and said to them, 'Be
fruitful and increase in number;
fill the earth and subdue it.
Rule over the fish of the sea
and the birds of the air and 
over every living creature 
that moves on the ground.'
Then God said, 'I give you every 
seed-bearing plant on the face of 
the whole earth and every tree 
that has fruit with seed in it.  
They will be yours for food.' "
When God created man , He 
made him as a ruler and king

so that he might lack nothing.
God prepared the garden of 
Eden for Adam and Eve.
Therefore, Adam and Eve were
not made to be poor, nor to live
in poor environment.  Yet, Adam 
and Eve and their descendants, 
we, have faced poverty as 
punishment because of our fall.
Therefore, poverty, famine, and 
hunger are God's punishment,
not His blessing.  The blessing 
of God is not poverty, but the 
poverty is the punishment from
God because Adam and Eve fell.
Genesis 3: 17-19 says, "To Adam
He said, 'Because you listened to 
your wife and ate from the tree
about which I commanded you,
'You must not eat of it,'
'Cursed is the ground because of
you; through painful toil you will
eat of it all the days of your life.
It will produce thorns and thistles 
for you, and you will eat 
the plants of the field.  By the 
sweat of your brow you will 
eat your food until you return 
to the ground, since from it 
you were taken; for dust you 
are, and to dust you will return.'
Take a look!  Poverty is God's
judgment, curse, and punishment,
not blessing.  2 Corinthians 8: 9
says, "For you know the grace of 
our Lord Jesus Christ, that though 
He was rich, yet for your sakes 
He became poor, so that you through 
His poverty might become rich." 
Jesus, who was rich God, came 
as a man and grew up 
at a poor carpenter's household 
in Nazareth.
Even after the Lord began 
His ministry, He bore poverty.
The Bible clearly said, "Through 
His poverty, you will be rich.
Galatians 3: 13 reads, "Christ redeemed
us from the curse of the law by
becoming a curse for us, for it is
written: "Cursed is everyone who
is hung on a tree. He redeemed
us in order that the blessing 
given to Abraham might come to 
the gentiles through Jesus Christ."
This means that Jesus destroyed
curse of sin by His body.
Jesus Christ came to pay 
the penalty of poverty and curse 
resulted from the fall of Adam 
and Eve and to give blessing 
instead of poverty, and the prosperity 
of Abraham instead of curse.
When Jesus was crucified, 
He cried, "It is finished," 
which means He proclaimed 
He gave us true release and 
freedom from poverty and curse.
Therefore, what God has given 
us grace, love, freedom, and 
release in Christ, means 
that we are released 
from poverty and curse.
So, may you believe that everything
will go well with you in Jesus.
Secondly, Jesus proclaimed that He
came to give complete freedom 
and liberty to the prisoners.
God is absolute freedom.
God has never been shackled
by anyone and can't be, for He 
is omniscient, omnipotent, and 
omnipresent.  Since man was
created in the image of God,
the man can't be bound by
anything while he worships God
and lives a God-centered life.
He is not a captive, but 
after he was away from God, 
he became a captive of uneasi-
ness, fear, despair, devil, sin, 
sickness, poverty, hatred, death, 
and many destructive powers.
The devil binds man with anxiety, 
fear, despair, sin, sickness, 

poverty, hatred, and death.
As a captive becomes a slave to
the one who has taken the captive,
so we have become captives of
devils, but we misunderstand it 

and live as if we were free.
In fact, who is really free 
in his life?
Man is born a slave and lives
as a slave to the devil.
Jesus judged all these hostile
powers on the cross and defeated 
them through resurrection, and now 
He is a great power of freedom.
Colossians 2: 14-15 says, 
"Having canceled the written 
code, with its regulations, that 
was against us and that stood 
opposed to us; He took it away, 
nailing it to the cross.  And 
having disarmed the powers 
and authorities, he made a public 
spectacle of them, triumphing 
over them by the cross."
The Lord nailed the written code,
that pressed us, on the cross, 
and triumphed over the power
and authority of the devil, 
and gave us release and freedom.
We have no power in us.
We don't have the power
to set us free and released.
However, when we receive Jesus, 
who has brought us release and 
freedom, as our Savior, He will 
give us true release and freedom.
I've heard of an American woman'
testimony.  She began drinking
at 16 and became a drunkard.
She was violated by many men
and met with a bad boyfriend
and became a drug addict.
She bore a baby, but while 
she was giving her baby suck, 
she fell on her face for her baby 
to be choked and die.

She was too miserable.  
She drank from early morning
to evening and then took drugs.
She wanted to be freed from
mental troubles and entered
the hospital and took medicine
and tried all the popular remedies,
but she couldn't be released.
Then her friend has a car accident
and came to church for funeral
and was moved by sermon.
She thought that she might 
be free. So she attended 
the Sunday service 
at the pastor's church.  
During the service, she saw people
praising God with singing in joy.
She wanted to be like them.
She heartily wished, so when 
the pastor said about the convert,
she came up to the alter
and earnestly repented and
said to the Lord, "Lord, I can't
get out of alcoholism by myself,
I'm a sinner and captive. Lord, 
please save me."  She heartily 
seek the Lord and cried to Him.
Then the power of the Lord
began to work in her heart.
She prayed depending on Jesus
and her alcoholism went away.
She was released from 
alcoholism and drug addiction, 

and so was her husband.
Both of them completely became
new as victors in Christ.
I have read an article about
their testimony in a magazine.
The Bible says, "The Son of 
God came to destroy 
the work of the devil."  
John 8: 32 reads,
"You will know the truth, 
and the truth will set you free."
Therefore, if our Lord, Jesus, 
comes in us, He works 
to make us free completely 
from captives.
Since Christ works in us with
the power of freedom and release,
we can be released from sin, 
the devil, and curse.
Thirdly, the Lord proclaimed 
recovery of sight for the blind.
Man had two kinds of eyes:
spiritual and physical eyes.
When we were first made,
our spiritual eyes were opened
and we saw God and spiritual
realm with fellowships with Him.
In the cool of evening, Adam 
and Eve walked talking with Him.
Yet, because of sin, the spirit
were away from them.
Then they couldn't see spiritual 
realm and had no friendship.
As a result, we became slaves
to sense, lust, and greed.
1 John 2: 16 says, "Everything
in the world---the cravings of
sinful man, the lust of his eyes
and the boasting of what he has
and does--- comes not from
the Father but from the world."
Likewise, Adam and his descendants
were dead in spirit and has belonged
to the world as slaves to lust.
However, Jesus came and make
us born again in spirit 
and opened our spiritual eyes.
The Lord was judged in our 
places and broke the wall between 
God and us, and spiritual realm 
return to us again.
John 3: 5 says, "Jesus answered,
'I tell you the truth, no one can
enter the kingdom of God unless
he is born of water and the spirit."
2 Corinthians 5: 17 reads, 
"Therefore, if anyone is in Christ, 
he is a new creation; the old 
has gone, the new has come!'
In Jesus, the old and the physical
are created to the spiritual.

They are born again.
After they are born spiritual,
they open their spiritual eyes.
Then they recognize God; 
the Holy Spirit, the Comforter;
the Bible, the Word of God;
and they get to know that
this world will be judged 
by God and burned, 
and the new heave and earth 
]and new Jerusalem will come.
This truth and understanding
are clear to those who opened
the spiritual eyes, but to those 
who are dead in spirit, all 
those things are from the 
foolish and ignorant and dull.
For the message of the cross 
is foolishness to those who are 
perishing, but to us who are 
being saved it is the power 
and wisdom of God.
That's why only those who are
open to the spiritual eyes
can see the spiritual realm.
Jesus came and granted us
complete release and freedom
from spiritual darkness.
So we have not only the present 
world and also the everlasting 
future praising it with hope.
Fourthly, Jesus proclaimed to 
release the oppressed. Before fall, 
man was not oppressed, for he 
was not weary and burdened.
The grass pressed under the rock
during winter can't grow in spring.
If we move the rock, 
we see the grass yellow.  
Yet, some time later, it receives 
sunshine and grows green.
In this way, if man is oppressed
by the devil, his spirit, mind, 
body, and life can't grow.
He looks yellow.  
For the devil comes only 
to steal and kill and destroy.
All the people are pressed by
the devil so they are depressed.
Jesus came to bear the burden
and sickness of man 
and to give him freedom.
Matthew 11: 28-30 says, 
"All you, who are weary and 
burdened, come to Me, and I
will give you rest, for I am
gentle and humble in heart.
Take My yoke upon you and
learn from Me, and you will 
find rest for your souls,
for My yoke is easy and 
My burden is light."
Jesus said that He made us
free and released through 
the yoke of the cross.
Acts 10: 38 tells us, 
"How God anointed Jesus of
Nazareth with the Holy Spirit
and power, and how he went
around doing good and healing 
all who were under the power 
of the devil."  Therefore, today
the devil presses people 
spiritually and physically 
and takes their power.
Likewise, the devil steals,
kills, and destroys people.
The only way for us to have
true release and freedom from
this press and have abundant 
life with the fullness of 
the Holy Spirit, is to accept 
Jesus as our Savior.
If Jesus comes, He makes 
the oppressed free.  
For the strong
can defeat the weak.
Jesus is the strongest one 
who has all the authorities 
under heaven and earth.
Since Christ was sacrificed 
and paid the penalty for us,
He defeated the devil and threw 
away the burden, and He granted 
us true release and freedom.

After they entered the land of
Canaan, they gave sin offerings
in 49 years after seven times of
seven-year Sabbath Year, and 
then proclaimed the Year of jubilee.
If on the Day of Atonement,
they proclaim the Year of jubilee
by blowing a horn made by 
the horn of a lamb, all the slaves
are released and the sold 
possessions are returned: it is 
the greatest day of the 
Israelites: the Year of jubilee.
