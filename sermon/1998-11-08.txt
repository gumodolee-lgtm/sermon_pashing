Sunday, November 8th 1998
Title : Forgiveness, Reconciliation and Love
Scripture Reading : Eph 2:12-22

Recently our society is filled with
conflict. What should we do to 
survive and overcome this extreme
discord? Where did our discord
come from first?  In fact, 
there was no conflict in heaven 
and earth which God created, 
for He is love and there can't 
be conflict among the creatures 
he made with love.  Love has 
only overflowing reconciliation.
However, the fallen archangel
caused the conflict. 
Isaiah 14:12-15 says, "How you
have fallen from heaven, O morning
star, son of the dawn! You have
been cast down to the earth, you 
who once laid low the nations!
You said in your heart, 'I will
ascend to heaven; I will raise
my throne above the stars of 
God; I will sit enthroned on the
mount of assembly, on the utmost
heights of the sacred mountain.
I will ascend above the tops of
clouds; I will make myself like
the Most High.' But you are 
brought down to the grave, 
to the depths of the pit."
Likewise, the archangel, Lucifer, 
the most beautiful one of the 
creatures that God made rose
to be equal with Him, then the 
conflict came into this universe.
There began severe conflict 
between God and the archangel
with the fallen angels. This 
conflict spread all over the human
world. God created Adam and  
Eve in His image and placed 
them in the Garden of Eden and 
showed them His tremendous love.
There was only righteousness,
peace and joy. Yet Satan came
there and planted conflict.
Satan tempted Adam and Eve 
to disobey and turn from God.
God said to them, "You can enjoy 
all the fruit in the garden but 
the fruit from the tree of the 
knowledge of good and evil. If 
you eat of it, you will surely die."
The fruit of the knowledge of
good and evil means the fruit
which makes someone the master,
that is, the sovereign. Only 
the sovereign and the Lord can 
decide which is good or bad, 
but the slave can't and all they
have to do is obey. It was the
tree of good and evil that tested 
Adam and Eve whether they 
obeyed God or not. Satan came
and planted discord. He asked
Eve, "Did God really tell you,
'you must not eat from any 
tree in the garden'? " Eve said,
"No, we may eat fruit from 
the trees in the garden but 
the fruit from tree of good and 
evil nor touch it, or we will die."
Then Satan said, "No, if you eat
that fruit, your eyes will be 
opened and you will be like God.
He is afraid that you will be
like Him." In this way, Satan
plated conflict saying, "God 
loves us. Why does he keep 
us from becoming like Him?
He is too jealous. How can 
He deal with us like this?'
Satan placed this sort of 
conflict into the heart of Eve.
She was distressed with that
conflict and finally ate the fruit
of good and evil and made 
Adam, her husband, eat it, too.
Hence, Satan made deep rebellion
and conflict between God and
man. Because of greed to be 
like God, man disobeyed Him
and was against Him and 
was troubled in his heart.
Adam and Eve ate the fruit of
good and evil and realized they 
were naked and hid themselves
among the trees. "Naked" means
their consciences were naked.
Since they disobeyed God and
sinned, their agony and discord 
in their conscience was too severe.
However, 2000 years ago our 
Lord Jesus Christ came to solve
this conflict with forgiveness,
reconciliation and love. 
Take a look at the cross! 
It has both a horizontal line and 
a vertical line. That symbolizes
discord, doesn't it? One line is
headed for the horizontal way,
and the other for the vertical way.
Isn't it discord? The cross on
which Jesus was hanging 
symbolizes the conflict in our lives.
On the cross of conflicts of 
individuals, families, brothers,
neighbors, society, nations, and
the world, Jesus was hanging.
Romans 3:25 reads, "God presented
Him as a sacrifice of atonement,
through faith in His blood. He did
this to demonstrate His justice, 
because in His forbearance 
He had left the sins committed 
beforehand unpunished."
Colossians 1:20 states, "Through
Him to reconcile to Himself all
things, whether things on earth
or things in heaven, by making
peace through His blood, shed 
on the cross." Jesus was hanging
on the cross not to solve our 
conflict with His power but with
forgiveness, reconciliation and love
through His sacrifice of atonement.
The conflict between God and
man was too terrible. Man rebelled
God and was cast out and He
cursed man, so man was doomed
to perish. There was the discord 
between God and man in that
like horizon and vertical line.
There was no way. Then Jesus,
the Son of God came and His 
body was torn and He shed His
blood on the cross so that God
had no discord with man. Since
our sins were judged through
Jesus, God forgave, reconciled
to us and loved us through 
Jesus' blood. When we come to
God, if we look to Him through
Jesus depending on His blood,
God's wrath and the wall of
enemy disappear and we can
reconcile to God and love Him.
Therefore, the conflict between
God and man disappeared through 
the torn body and blood of Jesus,
so God forgave, reconciled to
man and loved him. So did man.
There is no more conflict.
Now due to Jesus Christ who
reconciled us to God, we can
live with joy and give thanks 
to Him. What a wonderful grace!
Without Jesus as a sacrifice of 
atonement, we would have had
conflict with God and must have
abandoned forever. But thanks
to Christ, conflict disappeared 
and we are overflowing with
forgiveness, reconciliation and
love and we can rejoice in God.
He pours us His Spirit through
Jesus Christ so that we might
be filled with righteousness, 
peace, joy and eternal hope.
Moreover, through Jesus' sacrifice
on the cross, we are free from
the conscientious discord.
Who didn't commit sin?
Whenever we sin, our conscience
convicts and accuses us so
we stay up all night. Though
no one says anything to us,
our conscience rebukes us.
We are distressed in our
conscience and have conflict.
Then we struggle to remove
our conscientious distress, but 
our conscience accuses us more. 
Only when people remove 
conscientious conflict, they can 
have peace. Yet, as long as 
they have conflict in heart,
they can't stop being distressed
no matter how well they get
dressed and eat. How do they
get rid of the cross of their
conflict in their hearts?
Since Jesus Christ became a
sacrifice on this cross of conflict,
only when we accept Him as 
our Savior, our conflict will go
away. For Jesus' blood tells our
conscience, "For the sake of this
man I died. For the sake of this
man's sin, my body was torn 
and I shed my blood. Therefore,
For this man's sin I was judged,
and I forgave him so you, 
conscience, must forgive him."
Jesus Christ's sacrifice on the
cross makes our conscientious
accusation calm and makes us
overflow with forgiveness, 
reconciliation and love. 
Therefore, we should forgive,
reconcile to and love ourselves.
Through Jesus Christ's blood
on the cross, we can forgive
and reconcile with ourselves 
and agree with ourselves then
we can have peace in our hearts.
Romans 7:22-25 tells us, "In my
inner being I delight in God's
law; but I see another law at 
work in the members of my body,
waging war against the law of
my mind and making me a 
prisoner of the law of sin at 
work within my members. 
What a wretched man I am! 
Who will rescue me from this 
body of death? Thanks be to 
God through Jesus Christ our
Lord! So then, I myself in my
mind am a slave to God's law,
but in the sinful nature a slave
to the law of sin." Since this 
conflict in our mind is removed 
through Jesus Christ, we should
thank Him. Hebrews 10:22 says,
"Let us draw near to God with
a sincere heart in full assurance
of faith, having our hearts 
sprinkled to cleanse us from a 
guilty conscience and having our 
bodies washed with pure water."
Our conscience is cleansed 
through the blood of Jesus Christ
and we become one through the
Holy Spirit without conflict.
The conflict between God and
us also disappeared and we are
filled with peace then it brings
us true peace and happiness.
Then we can drive out our conflict 
we have against our neighbors.
We argue with our neighbors
for our selfishness and greed
and have discord with them.
How can we remove this discord?
Only when we crucify our selfish
mind and greed on the cross 
with Jesus and have forgiveness,
reconciliation and love, we can
remove conflict and make a new
relationship. All the people have
their self-centered mind, thought,
and greed. As long as they keep
those things, their conflict will
be more serious and have argue
on and on. We must follow what
Jesus did. Man rebelled God 
but God didn't. God was betrayed 
by man but man wasn't.
Yet God Himself came to us
and took the cross and removed
the conflict that He had with us.
Likewise, if we want to eliminate
conflict we have with our 
neighbors, either one should
take the cross. Unless either one
or both of them take the cross
and show forgiveness, reconciliation
and love, their conflict will never
cease. Our Lord Jesus said in
Matthew 5:39-42, "If some strikes
you on the right cheek, turn to
him the other also. And if 
someone wants to sue you and
and take your tunic, let him
have your cloak as well. if
someone forces you to go one
mile, go with him two miles. 
Give to the one who asks you,
and do not turn away from
the one who wants to borrow
from you." What does this mean?
We have conflict, argument and
discord because of just a little
thing in our lives. In order to
get rid of those conflicts, we
should take the cross with
forgiveness, reconciliation and 
love. We continue to have 
conflict in our lives, but unless
we first take the cross and 
show forgiveness, reconciliation
and love, we will never be 
reconciled with our neighbors,
nor will our conflict go away.
We have both little and big
conflicts in our lives: among
married couples, parents and
children, brothers, neighbors,
and social classes. Because of
these discords we are not happy
and our lives are destroyed.
Only when each of us take the
cross and deliver the message
of forgiveness, reconciliation and 
love, our conflicts will disappear
and happiness will come.
The most awful and everlasting
conflict between God and man 
was eliminated by forgiveness, 
reconciliation and love through 
Jesus' blood on the cross.
The conflict between our conscience
and our being was removed by
forgiveness, reconciliation and
love through Jesus' blood.
When we eat Christ's broken
body and drink His blood, our
conflict with God disappears.
Through Jesus' body and blood
we overflow with forgiveness,
reconciliation and love from God.
Furthermore, when we eat His
broken body and drink His blood,
we tell our conscience, "be quiet,
for I am forgiven and loved by
God because of Jesus' death.
You, conscience, must forgive,
reconcile with me and love me."
Human conflict is not only 
for yesterday and today.
That has been with us as 
disaster since man was made.
The greatest thing in Christianity
is that our God Himself was 
crucified on the cross of this
conflict. God who was hurt by
man sacrificed for him who 
rebelled Him and reached out
His hands of forgiveness, 
reconciliation and love.
Only when we deeply receive
the grace and love of the Lord
and crucify ourselves on the 
cross with Him and show 
forgiveness, reconciliation and 
love, our conflict will go away.
Instead, love will echo there.
There's no other way than 
the cross of Jesus Christ to 
bring us happiness in our lives.
We have to look to the cross
and pray, "Lord, you were 
hanging on the cross, so let 
me do that and die, so that 
through me forgiveness, 
reconciliation and love may 
be overflowing." May you 
pray like this continually!
