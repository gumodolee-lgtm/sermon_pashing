



The Conflicts, Forgiveness and Love


Today's life and world include 
conflicts. Our country has severe conflicts
in politics, economy, ideology, 
and in the whole society.
Today's social serious youth problems
are the one side of conflicts.
If we go further and see our homes,
we can also find conflicts there.
If we look in our inner lives,
we see many sufferings 
because of conflicts with ourselves.
How should we solve these conflicts?
Unless we solve the problem of 
conflicts, the words of peace, 
happiness and joy will be just vain.
Then where does this conflict begin?
When God made Eden and put Adam
and Eve there, there was no conflict.
God accepted Adam and Eve with 
His great love and grace so they 
lived with great peace and joy 
in the love and grace.
When the devil came into them
with sin, he brought conflicts.
The devil came and tempted Adam
and Eve to take the fruit from 
the tree of good and evil 
so that they became arrogant 
and wanted to be like God 
and to compete with Him.
When they refused to obey
and trust in the word of God,
the conflicts came between
God and man, in the heart of man,
and in the human history.
God is the God of love and justice.
Since Adam was against God 
and had conflicts with Him, 
He had to judge to carry out
love and justice, 
so the conflicts came into 
the heart of God.
God had to love but to hate,
He had to bless but to judge 
and curse. Didn't He have 
conflicts in His heart?
Likewise, the deep conflicts 
started between man and God.
However, to save man, God chose 
the people and the priest country.
He sent the offsprings of Abraham,
Isaac, and Jacob into Egypt.
He trained them for 430 years
and made them the chosen people
and intended to lead them 
into the Canaan land flowing 
with milk and honey through Moses.
Yet three million Israelites who 
followed Moses had serious conflicts.
They were against God in the wilderness
with unbelief, disobedience, complaints,
and grumbles. God wanted to love 
them but He couldn't, and He wanted
to lead them but they didn't obey,
He wanted to bless them but
they didn't deserve to be blessed.
So God had a deep conflict
in His heart.  Moreover, right before
they entered the Canaan land from
Kadesh Barnea, they sent 12 spies
to Canaan for 40 days and nights,
and they did totally different from
the will of God. They said, 
"The land we explored devours
those living in it with high 
mountains and deep valleys.
All the people we saw there
are of great size.  We seemed
like grasshoppers in our own eyes.
We can't occupy the land.
We'd better be back to Egypt."
They rebelled against the will
of God. There were severe conflicts.
God killed ten spies there.
He made His people wander in
the wilderness and hated rather
than loved, judged rather 
than blessed. He made His people 
destroyed there for 40 years.
After then God led His people
enter the Canaan land flowing
with milk and honey. 
Yet they became wicked in
unbelief, disobedience, and idolatry,
bringing conflicts into the heart
of God.  He is struggling because
of conflicts and also man is 
suffering from the conflicts 
with God and with his neighbors.
In Numbers 14: 26-28, the Lord
said to Moses and Aaron,
"How long will this wicked 
community grumble against me?
I have heard the complaints of 
these grumbling Israelites.
So tell them, 'As surely as 
I live, declares the Lord, I will 
do to you the very things 
I heard you say."
What a great conflict God has!
Judges 2: 11-15 says, 
"The Israelites did evil in the eyes 
of the Lord and served the Baals. 
They forsook the Lord, the God 
of their fathers, who had brought 
them out of Egypt. They followed 
and worshiped various gods 
of the peoples around them.
They provoked the Lord to anger.
In His anger against Israel 
the Lord handed them over to 
raiders who plundered them.  
He sold them to their enemies 
all around, whom they were 
no longer able to resist.
Whenever Israel went out 
to fight, the hand of the Lord 
was against them to defeat them, 
just as He has sworn to them.  
They were in great distress."
Here we can see that there is
a deep conflict between 
the Israelites and God.
Without solving this conflict,
God can't help judging and
destroying man.
Whether man is destroyed
or the conflict is solved,

man has no power before God.
So God sent His only Son, Jesus, 
to solve this conflict 2,000 years ago.
God came in human body and
took charge of all the human sins.
He was awfully judged before God
and forgave believers' sins, showed
His love, brought us reconciliation,
and solved the conflict.
To solve the conflict, there is no
other way than to judge its cause
and to be reconciled with forgiveness
and love to become one.
The cause that man made conflicts
with God is sin, isn't it?
The wage of sin is death.
If man is judged to solve his 
conflict, all will be destroyed.
Therefore the Lord bore the sin,
the cause of conflict, and died
on the cross and destroyed sin.
In I John 2: 2 we reads, 
"He is the atoning sacrifice for 
our sins, and not only for ours but 
also for the sins of the whole world."
That's why we should look at the 
forgiveness and love of the cross.
Jesus was hanging on the cross
of severe conflict.
The cross is the symbol of
conflict. If we draw the cross,
we see one line is going vertically
the other line is going horizontally.

That is the conflict, isn't it?
It is a horrible confrontation,
disagreement, and inconsistency.
On the cross Jesus was crucified.
Jesus, the true God and man,
broke His body and shed His blood,
forgave all the sins of conflicts
and brought love and reconciliation.
Jesus was hanging on the cross
without any power, but it was
not because He was weak or
powerless., but because He had
to save man from perishing 
and to solve the conflict.
He had to offer us forgiveness
and love.  Without forgiveness
and love, we can't solve conflicts.
If we say we are right by insisting
our own opinion and there are 
such people more and more, 
the conflicts will be more serious

and we will have destruction.
Without forgiveness and love,
the conflict is not solved,
but there is no forgiveness 
and love unconditionally.

The wrong must be punished.
Therefore, Jesus judged our sins
on the cross and as a result,
He showed us forgiveness
and love, removing the conflict.
1 John 4: 10 says, "This is love:
not that we loved God, but that
He loved us and sent His Son
as an atoning sacrifice for our sins."
Likewise, all the conflicts between
God and man are solved by Jesus.
Since our sins are judged and
God embraces us with forgiveness
and love through Jesus, we can
be reconciled to God.
Because we are forgiven and
loved by God and reconciled with
Him, He is our real Father 
and we are His real children.
Through reconciliation, we can
be with God and His Spirit 
comes and works in us.
Furthermore, the conflicts in us
and with our neighbors can be
removed only through the cross.
You know how severe your inner
conflicts are.  Although you want
to love, you have hatred.
That is your conflict.
You want to love your husband,
but you have hatred.
That is the conflict between
love and hatred, isn't it?
You want to love your wife,
you have hatred.  
That is your serious conflict.
Although you want to love
your children, you have hatred.
Though you want to love your
parents, you have a grudge and 
hatred. That is the conflict.
There is also the conflict
between blessing and jealousy.
If our cousin buys rice field,
we are jealous. So we say
we bless him outwardly, but
we have jealousy in hearts.
Great joy brings great sorrow.
In this way, we have continual
joy in our hearts and also sorrow.
That is the conflict.
We have the conflict of hope 
and despair. If we listen to 
the sermon at church, we are
filled with hope.  But if we
go home, we face hopelessness.
We are confused, for we don't 
know when we have hope 
and when we have despair.
There is also the conflict between 
positiveness and negativeness.
Right after we make up our minds
to be able, we think negatively.
We have the conflict of
positiveness and negativeness.
It is the same with our neighbors.
Though we love our parents 
and our children, we have 
conflicts continually.
Matthew 6: 12 reads, "Forgive
us our debts, as we also have
forgiven our debtors."
Therefore, only forgiveness and
love can bring 
reconciliation
for the conflict. 
We have no power of 
forgiveness and love.
It is in the cross of Jesus.
We can't forgive, but hate and
curse, for we have no power
to love. But when we look at
the cross and Jesus forgives and
love us.  He asks us to forgive
and love others, then we can
do that depending on Him.
If we pray and rely on Jesus
we can forgive and love.
Unless we forgive and love,
we are distressed.
Peter came before Jesus,
and asked, "Lord, Am I all right 
if I forgive seven times the one 
who sinned against me?"
The Lord said, "No, not seven
times, but seventy-seven times."
Today many people are suffering
from mental, diseases, family, 
economy, and physical problems. 
Why? For they don't forgive.
The Bible says, "Shouldn't you
have had mercy on your fellow
servant just as I had on you?"
And Ephesians 4: 32 says,
"Be kind and compassionate to
one another, forgiving each other,
just as in Christ God forgave you."
